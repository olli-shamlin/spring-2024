Session 6: Search & Help

While the previous sessions of this course provided an end-to-end overview of Internet and Cloud technologies, we have in many regards just touched the surface of the digital world. There is always more to explore and understand. The Internet itself is a trove of information on digital technologies including what they, how they work, when/why to use them, and how to use them. However, the challenge is often finding information that answers your questions in a way that makes sense to you and enables you to do more with the Internet successfully.

Given this is the last session of the course, we will also review key concepts covered in the course and retrospect on most and least valuable things we learned.

Aims and Outcomes

This session aims to

  1. Survey documentation for popular browsers and cloud services.
  2. Provide strategies for using search engines to find other sources of help.
  3. Explore the reasons some "help" references are not immediately or directly helpful and discuss approaches you can take in such situations.

Upon completing this session, you will be able to

  1. Find the "help" documentation for popular browser and cloud services as well as have an understanding of how it is organized.
  2. Use search engines to more effectively answer "what", "why", and "how" type questions.
  3. When a help reference is not immediately helpful, identify why and apply strategies to get value from it regardless.
