Session 5: The Cloud

Cloud technology emerged in the late 1990s and became fairly ubiquitous on the Internet in the 2000s. Most people use some facets of cloud technology and often without being aware they are doing so. It is common for people to have questions along the lines of

  • What is the cloud?
  • Where is the cloud?
  • Am I using the cloud? or Why should I use the cloud?

Aims and Outcomes

This session aims to

  1. Define what the cloud is.
  2. Explain the pros and cons of cloud technology.
  3. Demonstrate the types of commonly used cloud applications.

Upon completing this session, you will be able to

  1. Explain the basic differences between "desktop computing" and "cloud computing."
  2. Recognize when you are using cloud technology.
  3. Identify the cloud capabilities of the Apple, Microsoft, and Google.
  4. Assess if/when using cloud technology is beneficial to you.
