Course Aims and Outcomes

This course is designed to increase your digital proficiency specifically as it pertains to the Internet. Lectures presented over the course's sessions will give you a working understanding of how the Internet, the World Wide Web, and the Cloud work. While lectures will include technical discussions of how computers and software used with the Internet work, the lecture material targets a general audience. There is no prerequisite technical understanding needed for you to fully participate in this course.

Classroom exercises done with your laptop and a browser will reinforce the technical concepts presented in lectures. These exercises will give you exposure to the features and capabilities found in all browsers on the market today.

Aims

This course aims to

  1. Give you enough insight into how the Internet and the Cloud work for you to have a working understanding of what happens "behind the scenes" when you visit a site on the Internet.
  2. Give you a comprehensive and pragmatic understanding of common browser features.
  3. Give you a working understanding of how the accounts we often create with Internet sites work in a way that will enable you to manage yours more effectively.

Specific Learning Outcomes

Upon completing this course, you will be able to

  1. Explain what the Internet, World Wide Web, and Cloud are using terms and concepts anyone can understand.
  2. Define select technical terms and acronyms that are frequently used when discussing the Internet including but not limited to URL, HTTP(S), IP address, router, network, cookie, and hyperlink.
  3. Use common browser features with the Google Chrome browser and have enough of an understanding of how they work to enable you to effectively find and use the same features with other browsers if you so choose. Including (but not limited to)...
    1. Customize your browser
    2. Read URLs
    3. Use bookmarks
    4. Manage cookies
    5. Block ads (and marketing trackers)
  4. Manage your online accounts more effectively and assess when and how you can make them more secure.
  5. Know what it means to "use the Cloud" and assess if/when doing so is beneficial for you.

What if I miss a session?

Each session's content builds on content from sessions that precede it. If you miss a session, you will be able to find that session's material (e.g., lecture slides, exercises, and supplemental reading materials) on that session's detail page. You are encouraged to review the material and attempt the exercises for missed session(s) prior to the next session you attend.

Session Format

Sessions are two hours in length. There will be a ten-minute break near the middle of each session.

Each session will be a combination of lecture + Q&A and hands-on exercises. Some hands-on exercises will lead by the instructor with stepping through the activity at the same time; others will be written prompts that students will be given time in the classroom to attempt to complete independently. For the latter, the instructor will be available to assist students as needed.

You will need a laptop that you can bring to each session to complete hands-on exercises. You will also need

    If the Chrome browser is not already installed on your laptop, see the Installing Chrome page on this site for instructions on how to do so.

  • A Google account

    While you may already have a Google account (and you do if you use Gmail), you are strongly encouraged to create a new Google account to use during the course. Instructions on creating and deleting a Google account can be found on the Creating and Deleting a Google Account page on this site.

Can I use my tablet or smartphone instead of a laptop?

While Chrome can be used on smartphones and tablets in addition to laptops and computers, using a smartphone or tablet instead of a laptop in this course is strong discouraged.

The written material included with this course (slides, exercises, and additional references) is based on the laptop/computer version of Chrome. While the Chrome features and capabilities that will be discussed during the course are supported by the smartphone and tablet versions of Chrome, how you access and use them on a smartphone or tablet differs (in some cases, significantly) from laptop/computer version of Chrome.

While you will not be excluded from the course if you chose to bring a tablet or smartphone to sessions, you may experience challenges completing exercises due to the fact that course materials present "how to" instructions and examples using the laptop/desktop version of Chrome. Expect the instructor's capacity and ability to assist you in addressing differences between the laptop/desktop and smartphone/tablet versions of Chrome to be limited.
