Session 2: Browsers Part 2

You are probably aware that some sites collect information about you when you use those sites. Browsers collect and save information too. In this session, we will review the kinds of information browsers save, why they save the information, how a browser uses the saved information, and ways you can manage the saved information.

Aims and Outcomes

This session aims to

  1. Define the types of information a browser saves.
  2. Explain when and why a browser saves the information it does.
  3. Give you ways to manage the data your browser saves.

Upon completing this session, you will be able to

  1. Define what a browser bookmark is and perform basic related bookmark actions.
  2. Manage the information your browser saves based on your browsing history (including cookies.)
  3. Use Chrome's "incognito mode" to prevent your browsing history and cookies from being saved.
  4. Describe and use select browser settings (a.k.a. preference) that effect/influence the information stored by your browser.
