Session 3: The Internet

The first two sessions of the course include explanations of the basic interactions between browsers and internet sites. This session extends on those basics to provide insights into how the Internet works. We will explore what a computer network is and how such networks become part of the Internet. We will also expand our understanding of how browsers and sites interact by applying our new understanding of digital networks to the basic interactions between browser and sites.

Aims and Outcomes

This session aims to

  1. Explain basic computer networking concepts.
  2. Apply these concepts to understand what the Internet is and how it works.
  3. Define what an "internet applications" are and how they differ from the applications we install on our personal devices.

Upon completing this session, you will be able to

  1. Define what a digital network is and differentiate between the common types of networks.
  2. Apply this understanding of digital networks to your home network.
  3. Describe how devices connected to the networks are found by each other.
  4. Explain how the communication "traffic" generated by connected devices flows across the Internet.
